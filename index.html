What is the Prevent Strategy?

Prevent is a government strategy designed to stop people from becoming terrorists or supporting terrorism.

Early Intervention

Identifying and supporting individuals at risk before they become radicalized.

Safeguarding

Protecting vulnerable people from extremist ideology and recruitment.

Community Support

Working with communities, families, and institutions to build resilience.

Common Signs of Radicalization

Recognizing warning signs early can help prevent radicalization. Be aware of these behavioral changes:

Social Isolation
  • Withdrawing from family and friends
  • Breaking ties with previous social groups
  • Sudden preference for online interaction
  • Secretive behavior
Extreme Views
  • Expressing radical or extreme opinions
  • Showing intolerance of different views
  • Justifying violence
  • Using "us vs them" language
Online Activity
  • Accessing extremist websites
  • Sharing radical content on social media
  • Following extremist figures online
  • Spending excessive time online
Identity Changes
  • Sudden change in identity or beliefs
  • Rejecting previous lifestyle
  • Adopting new symbols or dress codes
  • Changing name or appearance
Behavioral Changes
  • Increased anger or aggression
  • Disengagement from education/work
  • Increased desire for travel
  • Possession of extremist literature
New Associations
  • Association with known extremists
  • Joining new groups or organizations
  • Secretive about new friends
  • Being recruited or groomed
Important: These signs alone don't confirm radicalization. However, a combination of several signs or dramatic changes in behavior should be taken seriously.

Your Report is Important

All reports are taken seriously and handled confidentially. You won't be wasting anyone's time, and your intervention could save lives. Early reporting allows professionals to provide support and prevent potential harm.
